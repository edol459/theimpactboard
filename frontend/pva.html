Novel metric

Possession Value Added

PVA is a basketball EPA model. For every possession, a gradient-boosted model predicts how many points an average team would score given the game state — the period, clock, and score margin. The difference between what actually happened and what was expected is the possession's value-added. PVA aggregates those deltas per player, normalised per 100 possessions, separating offensive credit (shooters and turnover players) from defensive credit (equal split across the 5 defenders who held possession).

How it works
Expected Possession Value
A gradient-boosting model trained on hundreds of thousands of historical possessions predicts expected points from four features: period, clock, score margin, and game-time fraction. Every possession gets a baseline — what an average NBA offense would score from that exact state.
Attribution
Who gets credit
Offensive credit flows to the primary actor: the shooter (made or missed FG), the turnover player, or the FT shooter. The difference between what they produced and what was expected is their possession's contribution. Defensive credit is split equally among the five defenders on that possession.
Interpretation
Reading the numbers
+2.0 Off PVA/100 means this player's possessions produced 2 more points per 100 possessions than a league-average offense would in identical game states. +1.5 Def PVA/100 means defenses they were part of saved 1.5 points per 100 versus expectation. Total PVA/100 combines both.
